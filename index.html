Interactive CPD Stop Analysis Map

Explore investigatory and gang-related stops in Chicago from 2016-2023

This interactive map visualizes investigatory and gang-related stops conducted by the Chicago Police Department from 2016 to 2023. The map allows users to explore trends and patterns in police stops across Chicago's districts over time. Use the year slider to examine how stops evolved during different time periods and under varying political leadership.

Key changes in Chicago's leadership during this period:

  • Rahm Emanuel (2011–2019): Policies during Emanuel's tenure included heightened police oversight following high-profile incidents and attention to police accountability.
  • Lori Lightfoot (2019–2023): Lightfoot's term emphasized police reform, community engagement, and budget reallocations aimed to address systemic inequities.
  • Brandon Johnson (2023–present): Johnson's focus includes reshaping public safety initiatives and investing in alternative approaches to community well-being.

    • Use the buttons to switch between investigatory and gang-related stops, and explore the relationship between policing strategies and changes in mayoral leadership or policies. This tool highlights how public safety practices have adapted over time, providing insights into broader socio-political trends in Chicago.
